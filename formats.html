Magic: The Gathering Formats

What are Formats in Magic: The Gathering?

In Magic: The Gathering, a format is the type of game mode you will be playing and it defines:

  • How big your deck must be
  • Which cards you're allowed to use
  • How many players take part
  • Whether the game is competetive

Some formats are great for learning, others for collecting, and some for organised tournament play.

Constructed Formats

You build your deck before the game using your own cards.

Standard

Best for: New players, learning the basics, local game nights.

  • Deck Size: Minimum 60 cards
  • Copies allowed: Up to 4 of each card (except basic lands)
  • Card pool: Only the most recent sets (rotates every year)
  • Players: Usually 2

Standard keeps the game fresh by regularly changing which sets are legal. Because the card pool is smaller, it's easier for beginners to understand and less overwhelming.

Modern

Best for: Players who want deeper strategies without going fully old-school

  • Deck Size: Minimum 60 cards
  • Copies allowed: Up to 4 of each card (except basic lands)
  • Card pool: Most cards from 2003 onwards
  • Players: Usually 2

Modern is faster and more powerful than Standard. Games can end quickly, and decks often focus on very specific strategies.

Legacy & Vintage

Best for: Long-time players and collectors

  • Deck Size: Minimum 60 cards
  • Copies allowed: Up to 4 of each card (except basic lands)
  • Card pool: Almost all Magic cards ever printed

These formats use extremely powerful cards and can be very expensive. Vintage allows nearly everything but restricts some cards to single copy instead of banning them outright.

Commander (EDH)

Best for:Casual play, multiplayer games, social Magic

This is one of the most popular formats in the world.

  • Deck Size: Exactly 100 cards
  • Copies allowed: Only 1 copy of each card (except basic lands)
  • Commander: A legandary creature that leads your deck
  • Card colours: Every card must match your commander's colour identity
  • Starting life: 40 life per player
  • Players: Usually 3 - 5

Commander games are slower, splashier, and more social. Politics, alliances, and big dramatic plays are common. It's ideal for relaxed play and storytelling.

Limited Formats

You build your deck during the event using opened booster packs.

Draft

Best for: Learning card evaluation and deck-building skills

  • Players sit in a group and open booster packs one at a time
  • Cards chosen one by one and passed around the table
  • Decks must have at least 40 cards
  • Basic lands are provided

Draft tests your ability to adapt and read what other players are doing.

Sealed

Best for: New players, prerelease events

  • Each player opens a set number of booster packs
  • Build a deck of at least 40 cards from those cards
  • Basic lands are provided

Sealed is slower and more forgiving than Draft, making it ideal for beginners and friendly events.
