Magic: The Gathering Formats

What are Formats in Magic: The Gathering?

In Magic: The Gathering, a format defines:

  • How big your deck must be
  • Which cards you're allowed to use
  • How many players take part
  • Whether the game is competetive

Some formats are great for learning, others for cellecting, and some for organised tournament play.

Constructed Formats

You build your deck before the game using your own cards.

Standard

Best for: New players, learning the basics, local game nights.

  • Deck Size: Minimum 60 cards
  • Copies allowed: Up to 4 of each card (except basic lands)
  • Card pool:Only the most recent sets (rotates every year)
  • Players: Usually 2

Standard keeps the game fresh by regularly changing which sets are legal. Because the card pool is smaller, it's easier for beginners to understand and less overwhelming.

Modern

Best for: Players who want deeper strategies without going fully old-school

  • Card pool: Most cards from 2003 onwards
  • Copies allowed: Up to 4 of each card (except basic lands)
  • Deck Size: Minimum 60 cards
  • Players: Usually 2

Modern is faster and more powerful than Standard. Games can end quickly, and decks often focus on very specific strategies.
