Welcome to Magic: The Gathering

Introduction

Magic: The Gathering MTG or Magic for short, is a world renowned trading card game which was created by Richard Garfield and first published in 1993. It combines the strategy, creativity and imagination into a fantasy themed game played and enjoyed by millions of people. It has become so popular that they have even created an official online Magic: The Gathering Arena game.
The players take on the role of a planeswalker which is a powerful spellcaster who uses spells, creatures and artifacts to rain supreme over their opponents.
Whether you enjoy fantasy stories, problem-solving, or social games with friends, MTG offers something for everyone.

How to play MTG

MTG is typically played with 2 or more players, each player starts with:

  • A deck of at least 60 cards (other game modes allow for larger or small decks)
  • 20 life points

Players then take turns:

  1. Drawing cards
  2. Playing lands to generate mana
  3. Casting spells and summoning creatures
  4. Attacking opponents to reduce their life total

A player will rain victorious once they have depleted their opponents life total to 0, or by meeting special win conditions

The Five Colours of MTG

Each card belongs to one or more of five colours, each with its own philosophy and playstyle:

  • White - Order, protection, teamwork
  • Blue - Knowledge, control, trickery
  • Black - Ambition, sacrifice, power
  • Red - Chaos, speed, quantity
  • Green - Growth, nature, strength

These colours will shape how the decks play and what strategies you will use.

Types of Cards

MTG cards fall into several main categories:

  • Lands - Provide mana, the resource used to cast spells
  • Creatures - Can attack and defend
  • Instants - Powerful spells which can be cast any time
  • Sorceries - Powerful spells but can only be cast your turn
  • Enchantments - Permenant spells which stay on the battlefield
  • Artifacts - Magical objects with various abilities
  • Planeswalker - Powerful allies with unique abilities
